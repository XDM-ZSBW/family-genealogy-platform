Early Colonial Period (1700s)

1703

Birth of Deacon Isaac North

Deacon Isaac North is born, establishing the earliest documented North family ancestor. He would become a prominent religious figure in the community.

Key figure: Deacon Isaac North

Death of Deacon Isaac North

Deacon Isaac North passes away, leaving behind a legacy of faith and family that would influence generations of North descendants.

19th Century Development

Early 1800s

Birth of Abel North

Abel North is born, destined to become the patriarch of the Ohio North family line and a central figure in North family genealogy.

Key figure: Abel North
Mid 1800s

Marriage of Abel North and Elizabeth Jane Herr

Abel North marries Elizabeth Jane Herr, establishing the foundation of the Ohio North family branch. This union would produce several children and establish the family's presence in Champaign County, Ohio.

Key figures: Abel North, Elizabeth Jane Herr
1850s-1860s

Settlement in Champaign County, Ohio

Abel North establishes roots in Champaign County, Ohio, becoming a landowner and community member. This location becomes central to North family history.

Key figure: Abel North
1870

Birth of William Franklin North

William Franklin North is born to Abel and Elizabeth Jane, continuing the North family line. He would live until 1933, spanning multiple generations.

Key figure: William Franklin North (1870-1933)
Late 1800s

Growing North Family

Abel and Elizabeth Jane raise their children: Birdie Musette, Charles Lyman, Cora Barbara, Lucy Jane, Mary Francis, William Franklin, and Wilber Arthur North, each contributing to their communities.

Family: Abel North Family

20th Century Legacy

1933

Death of William Franklin North

William Franklin North passes away at age 63, having witnessed the transition from the 19th to 20th century and contributing to the continuation of the North family legacy.

Key figure: William Franklin North (1870-1933)
